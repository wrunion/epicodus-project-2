Free and Affordable Resources

There's a lot of learning and networking that you can do on your own, without spending a dime! Free Code Camp, W3Schools, Treehouse, Code Academy, and Coursera all have an abundance of free and affordable resources. Yes, the certification often costs more, but most of the curriculum is available for free - and Coursera offers scholarships for most of their classes.

There are also several online learning sites, such as Udemy, where you can purchase quality, in-depth programming courses for less than $20. That's a far cheaper way to explore a new skill! They also come with course completion certificates, so along with the new projects you'll have in your portfolio, you'll have a new certification to list on your resume!

Finally, Meetup.com is a great place to start networking. Over 50% of new jobs are never publicly posted, so get out there, find the tech networking groups in your area, and start shaking some hands!

You might be interested in The PDX Code Guild!

The PDX Code Guild offers full and part-time immersive bootcamp programs in Portland, OR. Unlike the other bootcamps in town, they focus primarly on Python, and they offer an Advanced JavaScript course about Node and React. They also offer needs-based scholarships, which helps with the hefty cost of tuition.

You might be interested in Epicodus!

Epicodus provides both full and part-time classes in full-stack web design, although it's full program is only available full-time.

All of its curriculum is available for free, online, which is a nice feature. They also stand out among Portland schools as the only one to offer a 5 week internship at the end of their full time code camp.

You might be interested in Alchemy Code Lab!

Alchemy Code Lab is another full-time, immersive bootcamp in Portland, Oregon, taught in person in their downtown location. Like its local competitors, Alchemy Code Lab teaches the fundamentals of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript in just a handful of months, promising salaries of over $70k upon graduation.

In the Who We Are section of their website, Alchemy states that they value diversity, and intentionally welcome women, LGBTQ people, and veterans, among others. They also partner with local organizations to make scholarships possible for these diverse populations.

You might be interested in The Tech Academy!

While The Tech Academy has physical locations in both Portland and Seattle, they also offer online, self-paced bootcamps that you can take from anywhere in the world. They offer a wide variety of languages, and the option to learn at home, at your own speed.

All that being said, is it really worth the $8-20k you're about to drop on a code course, especially when there are so many free and affordable alternatives? That's up to you!
